Ozempic and Wegovy hit one receptor (GLP-1). Mounjaro hits two (GLP-1 + GIP). Retatrutide is the first compound to hit all three — GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon — at the same time.

That third receptor matters because it's the one that increases how many calories your body burns at rest. Older compounds just suppress appetite. Retatrutide suppresses appetite and speeds up your metabolism.

Will it work for me if nothing else has?

If diets, gym memberships, or older GLP-1s haven't worked for you, that's actually a strong signal Retatrutide could.

The reason older approaches fail isn't willpower — it's that most people's bodies have multiple hunger pathways that need to be addressed. Single-pathway tools (diet, Ozempic) only touch part of the problem. Retatrutide is the first compound built to address all three at once.
